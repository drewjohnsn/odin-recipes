Tomata Sauce by Marcella Hazan

After cooking for a while, you start to pick up on a few dishes that the cheap and easy method just isn't worth the convenience. For me, that was tomato sauce. While this takes a significantly longer time than a jar of Prego, the results are absolutely worth it â€“ and most of the time is inactive anyway!

I can not overstate how important good tomatoes are for this recipe. USE GOOD SAN MARZANO OR SAN MARZANO STYLE TOMATOES.

Ingredients

  • 28oz can of good San Marzano tomatoes
  • 5 Tbsp of Butter
  • 1 medium onion
  • 1 pound of pasta (I like spaghetti or fettucine, but sub your favorite shape and it should be fine!

Instructions

  1. Add your 28oz can of tomatoes to a saucepot over medium heat.
  2. Split your onion in half, remove stems and skins, add to the pot along with 5 tbsps of butter.
  3. Add a pinch of salt and bring to a simmer
  4. Simmer for 45 minutes, stirring occasionally. If the pot gets too hot or it bubbles too much, slightly reduce the heat.
  5. Once they begin to soften, crush the tomatoes on the wall of the saucepan.
  6. After 45 minutes have elapsed, taste the sauce for seasoning. Add any necessary salt, remove the onions from the pan.
  7. Opt. use an immersion blender to create a finer consistency.
  8. Remove sauce from the saucepan and palce in a skillet. Keep warm over medium-low heat.
  9. Boil a pound of pasta in very salty water until al dente, usually 1-2 minutes before package instructions.
  10. Using tongs, add your pasta to the pan with the sauce and toss to evenly coat the pasta.
  11. Serve in a warm bowl with grated parmasan cheese.
